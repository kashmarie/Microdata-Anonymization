Research Domain

A comprehensive breakdown of the background, problem statement, and methodology of our study.

Literature Survey

Extensive literature has been reviewed in the field of Statistical Disclosure Control (SDC) and Privacy-Preserving Data Publishing (PPDP). Early works primarily focused on simple suppression and generalization. However, models like k-anonymity (Sweeney, 2002), l-diversity (Machanavajjhala et al., 2007), and t-closeness (Li et al., 2007) have become the gold standard for preventing re-identification and attribute disclosure.

Recent advancements incorporate Differential Privacy and Machine Learning-based synthesis, though deterministic anonymization remains preferred for census data due to its need for exactness in specific non-sensitive aggregations.

Research Gap

While many generic anonymization tools exist, there is a significant gap in tailored solutions specifically designed for the hierarchical and deeply correlated nature of the Department of Census and Statistics datasets. Existing methods often result in excessive information loss (degrading utility for researchers) or fail to account for complex multi-dimensional vulnerabilities.

Research Problem

"How to effectively anonymize multi-dimensional census microdata to prevent identity and attribute disclosure, while preserving maximum statistical utility for socio-economic research and policymaking?"

Research Objectives

  • To evaluate the risk of re-identification in current census datasets.
  • To implement and benchmark state-of-the-art anonymization algorithms (k-anonymity, l-diversity).
  • To develop an optimized, utility-aware anonymization framework tailored for demographic data.
  • To provide a user-friendly tool/interface for the Department of Census and Statistics to easily anonymize new datasets.

Methodology

Our approach follows a rigorous scientific methodology:

1. Data Profiling & Preprocessing: Identifying Quasi-Identifiers (QIs), Sensitive Attributes (SAs), and cleansing the raw data.
2. Risk Assessment: Calculating initial re-identification risk thresholds.
3. Anonymization Engine: Applying local suppression, global recoding, and micro-aggregation.
4. Utility Measurement: Quantifying information loss using metrics like IL (Information Loss) and Truthfulness.
